Code of Conduct (2018.07.15):

Members of Waffles and Code are expected to:
  |
  |--Treat all members with respect. We maintain a zero tolerance policy regarding:
  |  |--Harassment
  |  |--Abuse
  |  |--And generally aggressive behavior
  |
  |--Clean their dishes and workspace after each session.
  |  |--See "Leave No Trace" camping policies:
  |  |  |--https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leave_No_Trace
  |  |--But ignore 5 and 6 regarding campfires and wildlife.
  |  |--Also ignore #3 regarding human waste disposal. Do NOT dig any pits.
  |
  |--Read and agree to the 10 Commandments of Computer Ethics
     |--https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ten_Commandments_of_Computer_Ethics
     |--Source File: http://www.computerethicsinstitute.org/images/TheTenCommandmentsOfComputerEthics.pdf
     |--Thou shalt not use a computer to harm other people.
     |--Thou shalt not interfere with other people's computer work.
     |--Thou shalt not snoop around in other people's computer files.
     |--Thou shalt not use a computer to steal.
     |--Thou shalt not use a computer to bear false witness.
     |--Thou shalt not copy or use proprietary software for which you have not paid (without permission).
     |--Thou shalt not use other people's computer resources without authorization or proper compensation.
     |--Thou shalt not appropriate other people's intellectual output.
     |--Thou shalt think about the social consequences of the program you are writing or the system you are designing.
     |--Thou shalt always use a computer in ways that ensure consideration and respect for your fellow humans.

Additionally, officers shall:
  |
  |--Ensure that a steady supply of waffles (and coffee, if available) are on hand prior to the meeting start time.
  |
  |--Ensure that disrespectful or confrontational behavior is addressed.
  |
  |--Clean communal assets including:
     |--Whiteboards
     |--Temporary files and drives
     |--Cookware and cooking areas
